FEATURE ABLATION STUDY - CLINICAL INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

OBJECTIVE:
Demonstrate that current models over-rely on cognitive test (MMSE)
and global brain metrics, lacking regional specificity needed for
clinically reliable Alzheimer's diagnosis.

======================================================================
RESULTS SUMMARY
======================================================================

Without MMSE:
  Average Accuracy: 0.8095
  Drop from Baseline: 0.0595 (6.8%)

Without Global Brain Metrics:
  Average Accuracy: 0.8294
  Drop from Baseline: 0.0397 (4.6%)

Without MMSE + Global:
  Average Accuracy: 0.7659
  Drop from Baseline: 0.1032 (11.9%)
  ⚠️  WARNING: >10% drop without global brain metrics
      Models rely on non-specific atrophy measures
      Cannot distinguish Alzheimer's from other causes

Only Demographics:

  Drop from Baseline: 0.1032 (11.9%)

Only MMSE:

======================================================================
CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS
======================================================================

1. OVER-RELIANCE ON COGNITIVE TEST (MMSE):
   - Performance drops 6.0% without MMSE
   - Models are learning: 'Low MMSE → Dementia'
   - Problem: MMSE measures cognitive impairment (the outcome)
   - Cannot detect early Alzheimer's (when MMSE is still normal)
   - Not adding diagnostic value beyond clinical assessment

2. NON-SPECIFIC GLOBAL BRAIN METRICS:
   - Performance drops 4.0% without global volumes
   - Models use total brain size/atrophy (nWBV, eTIV, ASF)
   - Problem: Many conditions cause global atrophy
   - Cannot distinguish Alzheimer's from:
     • Normal aging
     • Vascular dementia
     • Frontotemporal dementia
     • Other neurodegenerative diseases

3. MISSING ALZHEIMER'S-SPECIFIC FEATURES:
   Current models DO NOT measure:
   ❌ Hippocampal atrophy (earliest and most specific sign)
   ❌ Entorhinal cortex atrophy (preclinical marker)
   ❌ Medial temporal lobe pattern (diagnostic hallmark)
   ❌ Regional atrophy asymmetry (staging information)
   ❌ Cortical thickness patterns (early detection)

======================================================================
CONCLUSION
======================================================================

Current models achieve high accuracy by:
1. Predicting cognitive impairment from cognitive tests (circular)
2. Using non-specific global brain atrophy measures

These models CANNOT be clinically relied upon because:
❌ Cannot detect early/preclinical Alzheimer's disease
❌ Cannot distinguish Alzheimer's from other dementias
❌ Cannot identify Alzheimer's-specific atrophy patterns
❌ Over-rely on outcome measures rather than causal pathology

RECOMMENDATION:
✅ Extract regional volumetric features from OASIS MRI images
✅ Focus on Alzheimer's signature regions:
   - Hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, medial temporal lobe
   - Posterior cingulate, precuneus, parietal cortex
✅ Retrain models with clinically-informed regional features
✅ Validate against clinical diagnostic criteria
